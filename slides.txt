# ---- Slide 5: The problem ----
s5.kicker: The Hidden Second Shift
s5.title:  One incident.|A stack of *agencies.*
s5.lede:   The same night gets reported again and again: county sheriff, local PD, EMS, the state. Every agency wants its own form, its own fields, its own order.
s5.cards:  Same data :: Names, times, address, narrative, keyed in by hand for every single form. || Different templates :: Each agency's PDF is laid out differently. No two ask the same way. || End of shift :: This all happens when the crew is most tired and least sharp.

# ---- Slide 6: The cost ----
s6.kicker: What It Actually Costs
s6.title:  Hours
s6.lede:   Per shift. Per responder. Time spent re-typing what already happened instead of resting, training, or being ready for the next call. Across a department, it's hundreds of hours a year lost to redundant paperwork.

# ---- Slide 7: The solution ----
s7.kicker: There's a Better Way
s7.title:  Meet *FireForm* 🔥
s7.lede:   A "report once, file everywhere" system. The firefighter describes the incident one time, and every required form fills itself, for every agency. It runs locally and is 100% open source. It's *submitted to the DPGA* and free for any department to adopt today.
s7.chips:  Report once | File everywhere | Nothing leaves the machine | Open source & free | DPG candidate
