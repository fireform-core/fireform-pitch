The Hidden Second Shift

One incident.
A stack of agencies.

The same night gets reported again and again: county sheriff, local PD, EMS, the state. Every agency wants its own form, its own fields, its own order.

Same data

Names, times, address, narrative, keyed in by hand for every single form.

Different templates

Each agency's PDF is laid out differently. No two ask the same way.

End of shift

This all happens when the crew is most tired and least sharp.

What It Actually Costs
Hours

Per shift. Per responder. Time spent re-typing what already happened instead of resting, training, or being ready for the next call. Across a department, it's hundreds of hours a year lost to redundant paperwork.

There's a Better Way

Meet FireForm 🔥

A "report once, file everywhere" system. The firefighter describes the incident one time, and every required form fills itself, for every agency. It runs locally and is 100% open source. It's submitted to the DPGA and free for any department to adopt today.

Six Months Later

From a hackathon win to real-world impact.

Winning was the start. Here's the path from a hackathon prototype toward a deployed Digital Public Good.

The challenge

Reboot the Earth (UN · UC Santa Cruz · CalFire): open-source tech for wildfire-resilient communities and the responders who protect them.

6 months · ~6 calls

1st place → a Salesforce mentorship of ~6 monthly calls, turning the hackathon demo into software a department could actually run.

Where we are now

Open source (MIT), accepted into Google Summer of Code, and piloting with fire departments. Submitted to the DPGA, now under review.

What's next

Scale to real departments and a “Reboot in a Box” kit; external data APIs, an insights dashboard, and ML field detection.
