The story of one fire — and the second shift no one sees. This is how FireForm gives first responders their time back.

02:47 — The Call

Engine 14, structure fire.
Smoke showing.

A two-story home off the county road. By the time the tones drop, half the block is already awake. The crew is rolling before the dispatcher finishes the address — turnout gear half-on, adrenaline doing the rest.

03:02 — On Scene

They do the part
everyone remembers.

Lines pulled. A search of the upstairs. A family of four out the front door and accounted for on the lawn. Twenty-eight minutes of heat, water, and instinct. By 03:30 the fire is knocked down and the worst is over.

05:10 — Back at the Station

The fire's out.
The shift isn't.

Gear hung to dry, coffee poured, bodies running on fumes. Then the laptop opens — and the second shift begins. The part nobody puts on a recruitment poster.

The Hidden Second Shift

One incident.
A stack of agencies.

The same night gets reported again and again — county sheriff, local PD, EMS, the state. Every agency wants its own form, its own fields, its own order.

📝 Same data

Names, times, address, narrative — keyed in by hand for every single form.

🗂️ Different templates

Each agency's PDF is laid out differently. No two ask the same way.

⏳ End of shift

This all happens when the crew is most tired and least sharp.

What It Actually Costs
Hours

Per shift. Per responder. Time spent re-typing what already happened instead of resting, training, or being ready for the next call. Across a department, it's hundreds of hours a year lost to redundant paperwork.

There's a Better Way

Meet FireForm 🔥

A "report once, file everywhere" system. The firefighter describes the incident one time — and every required form fills itself, for every agency.

Report once File everywhere Nothing leaves the machine
How It Works

One input. Every form.

🎙️ Speak it onceA voice memo or one master description of the incident.
→
🧠 Local AIAn open-source LLM (Ollama) extracts names, places & details into structured data.
→
📄 Auto-fillThat one record fills every agency's PDF template automatically.

It runs locally — the data never has to leave the department's machine. And it's agnostic: it works with any department's existing fillable forms.

The Payoff

Give the shift back.

Hours returned to every responder, every shift. Built 100% open source as a recognized UN Digital Public Good — free for any department, from Cal Fire to a rural volunteer crew, to adopt and make their own.
